Before moving to San Diego, I did my M.S. at Stanford University, where I was advised by Prof. Marco Pavone. Before that, I was an undergrad at EPFL in Lausanne, Switzerland. I've received the Netherlands America Foundation Graduate Fellowship and am a Fulbright Graduate Scholar.

Research interests

My interests lies at the intersection of robotics, control theory, and machine learning. Specifically, I am interested in two areas (i) leveraging ideas from robustness in modular autonomy stacks to enable robots to generalize to novel environments and (ii) leveraging cognitive science insights on value of computation in robotics applications.
